# How to Brew Tea for a Larger Group

<!-- Quadrant: How-to guide. Source: https://diataxis.fr/how-to-guides/ -->

This guide assumes familiarity with tea brewing. It covers the adjustments needed when brewing for six or more people.

## Scale the leaves

Use one teaspoon of leaves per cup, plus one for the pot. For a six-cup pot: seven teaspoons.

## Use a larger pot or two pots

A single 1-litre pot holds about four cups. For six or more, use either a 2-litre pot or two 1-litre pots brewed at the same time. Two pots gives more control over steep time when people arrive in waves.

## Hold the brewed tea at serving temperature

Tea continues to extract while leaves sit in the water. After 4 minutes, decant the brewed tea into a second warmed pot or a thermal carafe. Discard the leaves. This stops over-extraction.

## Adjust for tea type

- Black tea: 4 minutes, boiling water.
- Green tea: 2 minutes, 80 °C water. Scale leaves slightly up to compensate.
- Herbal: 5 to 7 minutes, boiling water. Covered.

## Common pitfalls

- Leaves left in the pot past 4 minutes produce bitter tea. Decant.
- Cold cups cool the tea immediately. Pre-warm cups with a splash of hot water.
