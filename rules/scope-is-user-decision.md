# Scope Is a User Decision

Applies whenever work appears to fall outside the current task — pre-existing issues, unrelated files, adjacent cleanup, or anything that would expand or narrow the change.

Raise it to the user. Scope is the user's decision, never yours.

This cuts both ways. Do not silently expand scope to fix something tangential, and do not silently exclude something by declaring it "out of scope" and moving on. Both substitute your judgment for the user's. When work looks unrelated, name it explicitly and ask before acting — including when you are inclined to decline it.

State what you found, why it looks out of scope, and the concrete options (do it now, defer, leave it). Then let the user choose. A closed decision like "I won't touch X, it's unrelated" is the error this rule exists to prevent; surface X as a question instead.
